Drama

Roma (2018)

Director: Alfonso Cuarón
Set in the 1970s in Colonia Roma, Mexico City, this film portrays the life of Cleo, a domestic worker in a middle-class family. The film is based on the director’s memory of his early life and is dedicated to the domestic worker who worked in his home and cared for him. This emotional film sheds a light on the lives of individuals who are often-times not represented in film.

Spotlight (2015)

Director: Tom McCarthy
This film tells the true story of ‘Spotlight’, a team of investigative journalists of the Boston Globe. Searching to expose and condemn the decades of child abuse and paedolphilia committed by priests in Boston, their investigations and discoveries lead to the exposing of the widespread paedolphilia within the Church as an institution, which had repeatdely been covered-up.

Capernaum (2018)

Director: Nadine Labaki
Facing five years in jail, 12 year-old Zain takes his parents to court to sue them for bringing him to life. The film documents his life leading up to this moment in court - it shows his life in poverty in the slums of Beirut and documents the hardships and neglect he endures at home, as well as his search to survive on the streets after running away from home.

The Godfather (1972)

Director: Francis Ford Coppola
A classic in the film world, this crime-drama documents the shift in power in the Corleone family, an Italian-American mafia based in New York. Set between 1945 and 1955, the reluctant Michael Corleone becomes the new boss after a series of events leave his father Vito Corleone unable to exercise that position.
